Starting Over at 53: The Tools That Helped Me Build a New Income Stream

By Peter Lyons · True North Shift

At 53, I found myself looking at life with new eyes. Decades of hard work, a career in leadership, and the joy of two young daughters. But I needed change — not just financially, but in how I spent my time, my energy, and my presence as a father.

I didn’t want to keep trading time for money. I wanted to build something that could grow while I was reading bedtime stories, walking by a lake, or just sitting still for once. And I knew I had skills, experience, and a story worth sharing.

The Shift

Starting over isn't about erasing the past — it's about redirecting it. I decided to build income streams that aligned with who I am now. I focused on tools that were simple, low-cost (or free), and had real earning potential.

Here Are the Tools That Made the Difference

ConvertKit – Email Marketing Made for Humans

I needed a way to stay connected with people, share ideas, and eventually sell digital products. ConvertKit helped me create an email list without tech headaches. It's clean, simple, and designed for creators like us.

Frase – Write Smarter with AI

I use Frase to research and draft content like this. It helps me rank better on Google and saves me hours every week. Instead of staring at a blank screen, I get structure, keywords, and flow — fast.

Gumroad – Sell Digital Products Without the Hassle

Whether it’s a downloadable planner, a short guide, or a cheat sheet, Gumroad makes it easy to sell digital products. No storefront needed, and no monthly fees unless you’re selling.

Fiverr – Hire Help Without Going Broke

Starting over didn’t mean I had to do it alone. I used Fiverr to get help with design, tech stuff, and even small marketing jobs. $10 well spent can save you hours.

Notion – Plan Your Life Like a Pro

Notion became my digital HQ. I use it to plan content, map out goals, and keep track of income ideas. It’s free, flexible, and actually kind of fun to use.

My Advice? Start Small, Stay Honest

You don’t need to have it all figured out. You just need a reason. Mine was my daughters — I wanted to be present and build something they could be proud of. If you’re starting over too, just take one step today. Use what you know. Share what you’ve learned. And keep going.
